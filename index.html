✦ Why New Zealand

A nation built for curious minds

From globally recognised degrees to a lifestyle that's hard to match, New Zealand offers students an education experience unlike anywhere else.

🎓

World-Class Education

All 8 New Zealand universities feature in global rankings. Degrees are recognised worldwide, built on practical, research-led learning that employers across every industry respect.

🌿

Unmatched Quality of Life

Consistently ranked among the world's safest and most peaceful countries. Clean air, stunning landscapes, and a vibrant multicultural society make NZ one of the most liveable nations on Earth.

💼

Work While You Study

International students can work up to 20 hours per week during term and full-time during holidays. After graduation, a 3-year open post-study work visa lets you build your career in NZ.

💰

Affordable Tuition & Living

Tuition fees and living costs are significantly lower than the UK, USA, and Australia — without any compromise on quality. Numerous government and university scholarships are also available.

🤝

Welcoming Culture

The Māori principle of manaakitanga — hospitality and respect — is woven into everyday life. Students from every background feel genuinely at home across NZ campuses and communities.

🌐

Global Career Outcomes

NZ graduates are highly sought after globally. With strong ties to Australia, Asia-Pacific markets, and a booming tech sector, your NZ degree opens doors on every continent.

✦ Common Questions

Got questions?

Everything you need to know about studying in New Zealand. Can't find your answer? Ask our advisors.

Tuition fees for international students typically range from NZ$22,000–$45,000 per year depending on the programme and institution. Living costs average NZ$15,000–$20,000 per year. New Zealand is generally 20–30% more affordable than the UK or Australia for comparable degrees, and many scholarships can significantly reduce costs.
Most universities require an IELTS score of 6.0–7.0 (or equivalent PTE/TOEFL) for undergraduate entry, and 6.5–7.0 for postgraduate programmes. Requirements vary by institution and course. If your score falls short, foundation and English pathway programmes are available to help you reach the required level.
Yes — international students on a valid student visa can work up to 20 hours per week during term time and full-time during scheduled university holidays. This allows you to earn, gain local work experience, and offset living costs. Your partner may also be eligible for an open work visa in many cases.
Graduates who complete a degree of 2+ years at a New Zealand institution are eligible for a Post Study Open Work Visa of up to 3 years. This lets you work for any employer in any field — giving you time to build your career, gain permanent residency, or explore immigration pathways. PhD graduates may access even longer options.
Processing times for a New Zealand student visa are typically 4–8 weeks when applied online through Immigration New Zealand. You'll need an offer letter, proof of funds, medical clearance (if required), and a police certificate. We recommend applying at least 12 weeks before your intended start date.
Yes — there are numerous options including NZ Government Manaaki New Zealand Scholarships, university-specific merit awards, faculty scholarships, and country-specific grants. Awards range from NZ$5,000 per year to full tuition plus living allowances. Our advisors can help identify and apply for scholarships that match your profile.
New Zealand is consistently ranked among the world's top 5 most peaceful nations by the Global Peace Index. Crime rates are low, healthcare is excellent, and the country has a strong culture of inclusion and respect. All universities have dedicated international student support services and 24/7 welfare resources.
